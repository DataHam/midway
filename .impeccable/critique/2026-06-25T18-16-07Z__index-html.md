## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI/template-generated? Partly yes — it reads as competent 2010s corporate brochureware.**

**LLM assessment:** The hero is the canonical, over-seen composition: full-bleed photo + navy/60 overlay + centered white headline + a row of buttons. Nothing is broken, but nothing is distinctive, and — more importantly — it actively contradicts the brand thesis. PRODUCT.md's first principle is **"Route, don't list"** and two named anti-references are **Generic SaaS landing** and **Linktree link-dump**. The hero's 4 equal-weight CTAs (Danny Bio / Helen Bio / Contact Danny / Contact Helen) ARE a Linktree-style link-dump: same size, same priority, no point of view, no warmth. The voice "warm, credible, human — an introduction, not a pitch" is nowhere on the page; there are no people on it, just a logo, a tagline, and four buttons.

**Deterministic scan:** detect.mjs returned 5 findings, all typography: 4× "overused-font" on Montserrat and 1× "single-font." Both are weak here — Montserrat isn't even in the detector's own named overused list (Inter/Roboto/Fraunces/Geist/Plus Jakarta/Space Grotesk), and single-family-by-weight is a deliberate, documented choice (DESIGN.md's "Weight-Is-Hierarchy Rule") on a committed 2021 brand font. Treat all 5 as false positives / identity-preservation. The detector did NOT catch the real problems (routing IA, recognition gap, footer contrast) — those are judgment-level.

**Visual overlays:** Not available. Two Chrome browsers are connected (selection required) and the page depends on the external Tailwind CDN to render; rather than force a browser-selection detour for a visualization aid, this review is source- + token-grounded with computed contrast. No live overlay was injected.

## Overall Impression

Functionally solid and reasonably accessible (skip link, ARIA nav, responsive `<picture>`, focus rings, semantic landmarks) — but it's a generic corporate landing wearing the Tam-Tham palette, not the warm personal hand-off the brand calls for. The single biggest opportunity: **stop treating the two principals as four buttons and start treating routing as the designed centerpiece** — two clear person-zones, each with a face, a name, a one-line role, and an obvious "save contact."

## What's Working

- **Accessibility foundation is real:** skip-to-content link, `aria-label`/`aria-expanded` on nav and hamburger, Escape-to-close with focus return, visible `focus:ring-2` rings. Good bones.
- **Performance-minded hero imagery:** responsive `<picture>` with per-breakpoint WebP sources, eager hero / lazy elsewhere, explicit width/height. Thoughtful.
- **Palette discipline:** navy ground, yellow reserved for the contact CTAs, blue for bio routes — the color roles already mostly follow DESIGN.md.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] The routing is a flat link-dump, not a designed moment.** The hero's 1×4 grid of equal buttons is precisely the "Linktree link-dump" and "list, don't route" pattern PRODUCT.md forbids. **Why it matters:** the entire reason this page exists is to route a just-met contact to the right person; a flat grid makes that the visitor's parsing problem. **Fix:** split the fold into two person-zones (Danny | Helen), each a self-contained introduction with a clear primary action. *Suggested: /impeccable shape (rethink the routing layout), then /impeccable craft.*
- **[P1] The Biography/Contact split doubles choices and fights the mental model.** A visitor thinks "I want Danny," not "do I want Danny's biography or Danny's contact?" Four buttons, two labels per person, an ambiguous distinction. **Why it matters:** recognition friction at the one decision that must be instant. **Fix:** lead with the person; make **Save contact** the primary action and Bio a secondary link within each person's zone. *Suggested: /impeccable layout + /impeccable clarify.*
- **[P1] No recognition cue at the decision point.** The job is "which of these two did I just meet?" yet there are no faces or names on the landing — the profile photos exist (danny.html) but aren't surfaced where the choice happens. **Why it matters:** forces recall instead of recognition. **Fix:** surface each principal's photo + name + one-line role in their zone. *Suggested: /impeccable layout (or fold into the shape/craft above).*
- **[P2] Footer text fails WCAG AA.** `text-secondary-grey` (#535A60) on `bg-primary-navy` (#103248) is ~2:1; PRODUCT.md targets AA (4.5:1). **Why it matters:** unreadable copyright, and it's a systemic token misuse (grey is a light-surface color). **Fix:** white or Coastal Teal on navy. *Suggested: /impeccable audit.*
- **[P2] Generic, warmth-free hero with zero intentional motion.** The composition is templated and the copy ("Professional expertise in IT strategy…") is a category statement, not a warm introduction; there's no entrance choreography to give the page-load any brand presence. **Why it matters:** "AI made that" risk and a missed chance to set the warm tone in the first second. **Fix:** warmer framing copy that acknowledges the two people, plus one quiet, intentional load reveal (with reduced-motion fallback). *Suggested: /impeccable clarify + /impeccable bolder/animate.*

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (Confused First-Timer):** Lands on four buttons with no explanation. "Biography" vs "Contact" — which do I want? No helper text, no indication that Danny and Helen are different people you'd choose between. Hesitates at the only decision the page asks for.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** This is the dominant context — one-handed, just after meeting someone. On mobile the 4 CTAs stack vertically, pushing the relevant one down; the highest-value action (save the card) looks identical to a bio link and then routes through a Cloudflare Turnstile gate before the vCard. That's friction-on-friction for someone who just wants the contact saved before the conversation ends.

**Pat (Project persona — the just-met contact):** Danny hands Pat a V1CE card; Pat taps it and lands here. Pat wants Danny. Instead Pat sees the Tam-Tham logo, a generic tagline, and four buttons — two of which say "Helen." Mild "did I tap the right thing, and which one is Danny?" disorientation at the exact moment warmth and certainty matter most.

## Minor Observations

- **Token drift:** index.html renders via the `cdn.tailwindcss.com` script with an inline `tailwind.config`, while `npm run build` compiles `src/css/input.css` through PostCSS. Two sources of truth for the same tokens; they can silently diverge.
- **Hero subhead contrast is image-dependent:** Coastal Teal (#7DC2B6) subhead sits on the photo behind a navy/60 overlay. It's large text (≥3:1 needed) and fine over the navy mix, but a bright hero photo could erode it — worth verifying against the actual image.
- **Empty `bg-light-grey`** appears on sibling pages (danny.html) and is undefined in the config; not on index, but part of the same system hygiene.

## Questions to Consider

- If each V1CE card belongs to one person, should the tap deep-link straight to that person's page, with this shared landing as the fallback — rather than making every visitor re-select?
- What would the "introduction, not a pitch" version of this fold look like if the very first thing on screen were a face and a name, not a logo and a tagline?
- Is "Save contact" obvious enough to be the hero's single primary action, given it's the page's actual conversion?
