# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who have just met Danny or Helen Tam-Tham in person and tapped a V1CE
NFC business card (or scanned a QR / followed a short link). They arrive on a
phone, in the moment, often mid-conversation. They are prospective clients,
research collaborators, physicians, and professional contacts in and around
Calgary, AB. Two distinct principals share the brand: Danny (IT strategy,
digital transformation) and Helen (physician research). The job to be done is
to land, recognize who they just met, get to the *right* person, and walk away
with that person's details saved.

## Product Purpose

A digital business card and professional front door for tamtham.com. It exists
to make a real-world introduction stick: a tap leads to a fast, mobile-first
page that routes each visitor to the relevant principal (Danny or Helen),
conveys enough credibility to be taken seriously, and offers a one-tap save
to the visitor's contacts (vCard, gated by Turnstile verification). Success is
the visitor reaching the right person's page and saving the contact before the
conversation ends.

## Brand Personality

Warm and personable, professional without being corporate. Three words:
**warm, credible, human.** The voice is a real introduction between people, not
a company profile. It should feel like being personally handed a card by
someone you'd want to work with: approachable and relationship-first, but still
carrying the quiet authority of senior, established expertise. Emotional goal:
the visitor feels welcomed and reassured, not sold to.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing.** No gradient hero + feature grid + big-number metric
  template. This is two people, not a product.
- **Linktree / link-dump.** Not a bare stack of buttons with no point of view.
  The routing between people is a designed moment, not a list.
- **Flashy / over-animated.** No heavy motion or effects that undercut
  professional seriousness. Motion is quiet and purposeful.
- **Dated 2010s brochureware.** No stock-photo-and-stacked-sections corporate
  website feel.

## Design Principles

- **Route, don't list.** The core job is getting each visitor to the right
  person fast. The two-principal split is the central design problem, not an
  afterthought below a generic hero.
- **An introduction, not a pitch.** Every page should read like a warm personal
  hand-off. Copy and layout serve recognition and trust over persuasion.
- **Mobile-moment first.** The dominant context is a phone, one-handed, just
  after meeting someone. Speed, thumb-reachable actions, and instant clarity win
  over desktop richness.
- **Earn the save.** The contact-save is the conversion. Make it obvious,
  frictionless, and trustworthy (the Turnstile gate included).
- **Credibility through craft, not volume.** Authority comes from precise,
  considered design and restraint, not from more sections, more copy, or more
  effects.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (watch yellow `#F0D04C` and teal
`#7DC2B6` on light backgrounds — they fail as text colors and must sit on navy
or be reserved for large/non-text use). Full keyboard navigation with visible
focus states (already present via skip-link and focus rings), semantic landmark
structure, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for any motion. Design
for one-handed mobile use and outdoor/variable lighting, since the typical
arrival is a phone right after an in-person meeting.
